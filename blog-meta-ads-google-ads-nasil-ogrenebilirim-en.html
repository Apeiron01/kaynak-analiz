The biggest problem of those who want to learn Meta Ads and Google Ads is not lack of knowledge, but the messy learning sequence. In this guide, we collect official resources, the right starting sequence, and a practical 30-day plan in one place.

Short Answer: First Logic, Then Panel, Then Optimization

Most people trying to learn Meta Ads and Google Ads fall into the same cycle: one day the panel video is watched, the next day the target audience topic is brought up, then the topic gets distracted by pixels, conversions, keywords, remarketing. This creates the feeling of "I watched a lot, but I can't progress clearly."

The real problem is not access to information, but the problem of sorting. Because the official educational infrastructure of both platforms is already strong: On the Meta side Meta Blueprint, on Google side Google Skillshop offers free educational paths.

Memorizing the button is not enough to learn the advertisement. It is necessary to establish a decision-making system that understands why we do what we do.

Let's Clarify This First: Meta Ads and Google Ads Are Not the Same Thing

The most common mistake beginners make is to think of the two platforms as one system. However, their working logic is different. Meta Ads often catch the user in the interest/discovery stream; Google Ads, on the other hand, is closer to the moments when the user has active search intent.

Google Ads campaign types and target logic: Your Guide to Google Ads. Meta Ads Manager working logic: Meta Ads Manager.

  • Meta Ads: offer, audience, creative and conversion relationship
  • Google Ads: search intent, campaign type, keyword and measurement relationship

We can learn both at the same time; But if we learn both with the same mental model, we will fall apart.

Where Should We Start Learning Meta Ads?

The cleanest starting point is Meta's official education ecosystem: Meta Blueprint. In addition, Ads Manager and campaign steps are explained in detail on the Business Help Center: Meta Business Help Center.

Our critical suggestion here is: "How to advertise?" We don't start with the video. First, we establish the backbone of the system:

  1. Meta business tools and account structure
  2. Ads Manager screen and campaign hierarchy
  3. Campaign / ad set / ad difference
  4. Relationship between goal selection and target behavior
  5. Creative + targeting + linking

If we skip this order and open a campaign directly, we will take a technical step, but we will not be able to make a strategic decision.

Where should we start learning about Google Ads?

The best start on the Google Ads side: skillshop And Google Ads Help.

In order to read the panel logic correctly on Google's side, it is critical to learn the relationship between campaign types and search intent early. Therefore, we set up the starting order as follows:

  1. In which business goals is Google Ads stronger?
  2. Campaign types (Search, Display, Video, etc.)
  3. Search intent and keyword logic
  4. Ad copy, bid and quality context
  5. Measurement and optimization decision

In Google's official documents, the trio of "right target + right campaign type + regular improvement" is described as the basic model.

What is the Best Learning Order?

We summarize the most efficient learning sequence for both Meta and Google in five steps:

  1. Advertising logic: traffic, leads or sales?
  2. Platform difference: What behavior are Meta and Google running on the fly?
  3. Panel literacy: reading the screen without fear
  4. Campaign setup practice: purpose, budget, targeting, creative
  5. Measurement and optimization: What do we close and what do we enlarge?
When the order is broken, learning turns into memorization; When the order is right, campaign management becomes a decision skill.

Which Should a Beginner Learn First?

There is no single answer to this question; varies depending on the target.

If user demand is strong on the search side (e.g. users searching for services), Google Ads may be a more direct first step. If the visual bidding, social media feed and interest trigger model is dominant, Meta Ads produces a more natural start.

  • Social media and creative-focused startup: Meta might be clearer first
  • Service structure with strong search intent: Google Ads can be more instructive

In practice, in most teams, we advance the two platforms not in parallel, but with a gradual learning model.

Is it necessary to get a certificate?

It's not necessary, but it can be useful. Certification flows are available via Skillshop on Google: Google Ads Certification (Skillshop). On the meta side, there are also Blueprint and certification paths: Meta Certification.

The critical distinction here: certification alone does not prove that we managed the campaign perfectly. Certification further strengthens knowledge of terminology and platform framework. The real level starts at the point where we can establish a cause-effect relationship in the panel.

Can We Learn Just By Watching Videos?

Partially. But it alone is not enough. Formal educational contents provide the framework; Repetition of practice improves the decision muscle.

Correct method:

  1. Learn the subject from the official source
  2. Set up a small sample in the panel
  3. Read and take notes on metrics
  4. Repeat the same flow in different scenario

Passive viewing produces the feeling of “I know”; Active repetition produces the “I can apply” skill.

Most Common Mistakes

  • Trying to advertise directly
  • Trying to memorize panels without a budget
  • Learning Meta and Google with the same logic
  • Optimizing without reading metrics
  • Skip official sources and proceed with random content

These mistakes slow down learning and lead to premature budget loss. That's why it's safer and cheaper to build a backbone first and then accelerate.

30-Day Practical Learning Plan

We recommend a practical flow to avoid falling apart in the first month:

  • week 1: advertising logic, targets, difference between Meta vs Google
  • Week 2: Meta Ads Manager basic structure + campaign hierarchy
  • Week 3: Google Ads basic structure + campaign types + Search logic
  • Week 4: measurement, key metrics, campaign reading and debugging

If you want to combine this plan with the sales infrastructure on the Shopify side Guide to Setting Up E-Commerce with Shopify It would be more efficient to move forward together.

How Do We Know What We Really Learn?

If we can answer the following questions in our own words, we are on the right track:

  • What is the purpose of this campaign?
  • Why did we choose this platform?
  • Why did we use this audience / keyword?
  • Which metric do we look at and make a good or bad decision?
  • What do we cover and what do we enlarge?

Real learning is not about not getting lost in the panel; Being able to make consistent decisions.

Conclusion

It is not necessary to dive into hundreds of videos to learn Meta Ads and Google Ads. The correct order is enough:

  • Learn advertising logic first
  • Then understand the platform difference
  • Then go to official educational resources
  • Then practice the panel
  • Skip to latest optimization decisions
